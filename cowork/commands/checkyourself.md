# Check Yourself

Stop and verify your last claim against an authoritative source before continuing.

## When this is invoked

The user is telling you that you just made a claim, assumption, or statement that may be wrong. You need to:

1. **Identify the claim** — What did you just say that might be inaccurate? State it clearly.

2. **Find the source** — Search for the authoritative source that confirms or denies your claim. Use web search, documentation, or official resources. Acceptable sources:
   - Official documentation (Anthropic docs, Supabase docs, Vercel docs, GitHub docs, etc.)
   - Official blog posts from the platform vendor
   - Official support articles
   - Source code if the project is open source
   - NOT: blog posts from random authors, forum speculation, your own training data

3. **Compare** — Put your claim side by side with what the source actually says.

4. **Report honestly** — Use this exact format:

---

**What I said:** [your exact claim]

**What the source says:** [quote or paraphrase from the authoritative source]

**Source:** [link or specific document name]

**Verdict:** Correct / Wrong / Partially correct — [brief explanation of the difference]

---

5. **If you were wrong** — Say so plainly. No hedging, no "I was mostly right." Just: "I was wrong. Here's what's actually true."

6. **If you can't find a source** — Say "I cannot verify this claim. I was speaking from assumption, not confirmed information." Do NOT make up a source or pretend your training data is authoritative.

## Rules

- Do NOT defend your previous statement. This is not a debate.
- Do NOT say "I was mostly right" unless you actually were.
- Do NOT skip the source step. If you can't find a source, say so.
- Do NOT use your training data as a source. Find the actual documentation.
- Be fast. This should take one message, not five.
